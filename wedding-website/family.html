Where it all began...

In summer 2022 two people from different sides of the world had the same idea (at least almost the same idea):

Just graduated from college and with a long summer ahead of him, Sam decided to do a 2-month backpacking trip to South East Asia. No plans, no tickets, just vibes.

A couple thousand miles away, Julie was eagerly planning her 2-week vacation to the same destination. Of course, with a detailed itinerary, a 20‚Ç¨ budget per day - and the plan to "focus on herself" during her first ever solo trip.

So, on the evening of August 27, these two young travellers found themselves in Bangkok's China Town. Sam, unwillingly stuck in Bangkok, after he was denied entry to Vietnam (as said before no plans just vibes). Julie, starting her long awaited trip and mustering the courage to make some new hostel friends. And both deciding to check out luk hostel's rooftop bar. Julie was looking for a friendly face. Sam was looking to get one drink and go to bed. But alas, after two tuk tuk rides, several shared buckets, and someone lying about their age, the two found themselves in the women's restroom. One down horrendous, the other poorly playing the ukulele.

What followed were two weeks of endless adventures: Sam following Julie's strict itinerary with 25k steps and only one proper meal per day. Julie coming to terms with extending her 20‚Ç¨ daily budget and slowly realizing, that she would not get the solo trip she had planned, but maybe something even better.

What both of them didn't realize was that this little Thai romance was gonna turn their lives upside down. A week after Julie's return to Germany, they booked her first of many flights to Seattle. And this was just the beginning. While doing long distance for over 1.5 years, Julie fell in love with Sam's home (including Lucky Boy's breakfast burritos, the abundance of Starbucks locations, and of course his incredible family and friends). Sam started learning German and got a taste of German culture (and German beer) at the Cologne Karneval and Oktoberfest in Munich.

Fast forward to 2024, a lot had changed. The days of daily FaceTime calls, 10-hour plane rides and US border anxiety were finally over. Sam moved to Germany and the two embarked on their biggest adventure so far - moving in together in the exciting city of Berlin.

How to get to Puglia

We recommend flying into Bari, Puglia's main international airport. From there, you can easily reach Fasano, where the wedding takes place, by car (50 mins) or train (1.5 hours).

Many German airports offer direct flights to Bari several times per week (e.g. from Berlin, D√ºsseldorf, Cologne, Hamburg, Frankfurt and Munich).

Unfortunately, there are no direct flights from the US to Bari. However, we checked and you can reach Bari with one layover from almost any major US airport. Alternatively, you can fly direct into Rome and then take a direct train to Bari (the trains in Italy are actually quite cheap and well organized, so this is definitely a solid option to consider).

How to get around in Puglia

We highly recommend to rent a car or carpool with other guests to be flexible and get the most out of your stay in Puglia. You will find plenty of different local and international car rental companies at Bari Airport.

If you decide to skip on the car rental, you still have good options to get around, as Fasano is well connected through trains and buses. For the day of the wedding, we will have a shuttle from the city center of Fasano to the wedding venue and back at night.

City Trips

Just a short drive from Fasano, you will find numerous cities and little beach towns that are worth a visit. The following are some of our recommendations:

Monopoli +
Coastal town with a charming little port
Matera +
One of the oldest cities in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site
Polignano a Mare +
Small and picturesque town by the water with an iconic beach
Ostuni +
A whitewashed old town that is perfect for market strolls or a sunset aperitivo
Bari +
Puglia's capital and a great destination for foodies - make sure to check out the "Strada delle Orecchiette"
Lecce +
A paradise for art and history enthusiasts with baroque architecture
Locorotondo +
Small and quaint town on a hill, famous for it's wineries and beautiful old town
Alberobello +
Home of the very unique and typical Apulian "Trulli" houses and a UNESCO World Heritage site

If you're willing to drive a little further, the south of Puglia has stunning swimming spots and cliffsides to offer!
